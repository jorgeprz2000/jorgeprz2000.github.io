About

I research how artificial intelligence systems interact with human values, cultural differences, and social structures at the Ada Lovelace Institute, focusing particularly on the societal implications of advanced AI technologies.

My work combines quantitative analysis, qualitative research, and policy investigation to understand complex questions: How do large language models reflect or distort cultural values? What shapes public attitudes towards AI governance? How can we ensure AI development serves diverse communities?

I bring an interdisciplinary background spanning Computer Science, Behavioural Science, and Policy research. I completed my MSc in Behavioural Sciences at LSE (receiving the Hilde Himmelweit award for best in cohort) and my BASc in Computer Science & Psychology at UCL with First Class honours.

Current Research Focus

  • Cultural alignment in large language models and frontier AI systems
  • Public attitudes towards AI governance across political divides
  • Societal impacts of advanced AI assistants and delegation technologies
  • Copyright and intellectual property in the age of generative AI
  • Intersection of political psychology and technology adoption

Research Approach

I use a mix of experimental methods, survey research, computational analysis, and policy investigation. Recent projects have involved administering the World Values Survey to frontier models, analysing public attitudes data, and conducting stakeholder interviews across industry, government, and civil society.

Research & Publications

Current Projects

Cultural Value Alignment in Language Models
Ada Lovelace Institute & LSE Muthukrishna Lab

Leading research investigating how Large Language Models replicate diverse cultural values using innovative methodologies including stratified silicon sampling and multilingual prompting. This work administers the World Values Survey to frontier models and compares responses to country-level data through correlations, multidimensional scaling, and hierarchical clustering.

Chinese Internet Shock Analysis
MIT FutureTech Department

Implementing mediation analysis alongside SciBERT transformer models to analyse how the 2014 Chinese Internet shock affected data-intensive industries, working with Dr. Meicen Sun.

Recent Publications

Beyond Safety: Why Cultural Value Alignment in Language Models Matters
Forthcoming 2025 - Ada Lovelace Institute

Comprehensive report combining quantitative and policy research on cultural alignment in AI systems, examining why misalignment occurs and providing recommendations for policymakers.

How does political orientation shape public attitudes towards AI?
With Bruff O., Modhvadia R., Sippy T., Dutta A. - Forthcoming chapter, 2025, British Social Attitudes

Quantitative analysis examining how political partisanship and ideological strength influence public attitudes toward AI applications and governance approaches.

Delegation Nation – Advanced AI Assistants and why they matter

Policy brief explaining how advanced AI assistants work, their potential benefits and risks, and key considerations for policymakers.

Journalism and generative AI: data, deals and disruption in the news media?
With Jones B., Strait A., Parnell B.A., Horzyk A.M. - 2025, Bridging Responsible AI Divides

Workshop findings on the current state of news publishing regarding AI model training data, associated concerns, and potential solutions for news publishers.

What are Immersive Technologies?

Explainer of immersive technologies, how they work, different types, and policy considerations for decision-makers.

A Citizen Prosperity Index for east London: New evidence and a new approach to tackling inequalities

Quantitative analysis using simultaneous equation models to create prosperity measures based on longitudinal survey data in east London.
